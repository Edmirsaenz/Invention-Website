James Watt

James Watt was the the first inventor to make the first efficent steam engine which causes the first line of engine design

Using a separate condenser for the steam it was able to achive to same amount of work as the pervious engine with less power
This leads to future improvement later due to it being viable to use in factories and other places.

This is a A late version of a Watt double-acting
steam engine, built by D. Napier & Son (London) in
1832, now in the lobby of the Superior Technical
School of Industrial Engineers of the UPM (Madrid).
Steam engines of this kind propelled the Industrial
Revolution in Great Britain and the world.
