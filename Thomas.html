Thomas Edison invented a practical and long-lasting incandescent light bulb in 1879, improving upon earlier designs. His version used a carbon filament that could glow for over 13 hours, later extended to over 1,000 hours.

This breakthrough revolutionized indoor lighting and helped pave the
way for widespread electrification.
As more and more people used electricity, they saw the uses of it
and started to expand on it.
